# German Units in the 101st Airborne's Area — D-Day, June 6, 1944

Reference material for enemy force generation, AI behavior profiles, and encounter design.

---

## Overview: The German Defense of the Cotentin Peninsula

The German forces defending the eastern Cotentin Peninsula against the 101st Airborne
were a patchwork — not a cohesive army. Three divisions and one elite regiment shared
overlapping sectors, with wildly varying quality. This asymmetry is the key gameplay fact:
the player cannot assume every German unit fights the same way.

The 101st's operational area (Drop Zone C near Sainte-Marie-du-Mont, the causeways behind
Utah Beach, and the road network toward Carentan) sat at the junction of multiple German
command responsibilities. This created gaps in coordination that the airborne assault
was designed to exploit.

---

## 1. 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment (FJR 6)

**Commander:** Major Friedrich August Freiherr von der Heydte
**Strength:** ~3,500 men (full regiment, three battalions)
**Quality:** ELITE — the most dangerous unit the player can encounter
**Parent formation:** Attached to 91st Airlanding Division for operational control

### Profile

FJR 6 were German paratroopers — professionals who understood airborne warfare from the
other side. Von der Heydte was a Knight's Cross holder (1941) with Oak Leaves (1944), a
law professor turned combat commander, and a Catholic who refused to sign Hitler's
no-retreat order. He was smart, experienced, and dangerous.

The regiment was deployed to Normandy in mid-May 1944 specifically to counter expected
Allied airborne operations. German command recognized that Fallschirmjäger were "the best
candidates for anticipating the modus operandi of the Allied air-landing operations."

### Equipment

- FG 42 (Fallschirmjägergewehr 42): specialized select-fire rifle unique to airborne troops
- Standard MG 42 machine guns at squad level
- 8cm mortars at company/battalion level
- Panzerfaust anti-tank weapons
- Transport was critically poor: only 70 trucks of 50 different makes, forcing most of
  the regiment to march on foot

### Morale & Readiness

High. Von der Heydte kept his troops sharp with continuous patrols, night aircraft
observation exercises, and anti-airborne drills. He maintained one-third of his force
positioned in potential landing zones around the clock. He established 360-degree
anti-aircraft observation posts and deployed "Rommel's asparagus" (wooden anti-glider
poles) in likely landing fields.

Von der Heydte was the only German regimental commander fully alert on the morning of
June 6. He had just returned from a command meeting and personally recognized the invasion
was underway from his position at St.-Côme-du-Mont.

### Gameplay Implications

- FJR 6 troops fight aggressively and intelligently — they use fire-and-movement,
  set ambushes, and counterattack without waiting for orders
- They will recognize the player's airborne tactics (flanking, small-unit infiltration)
  because they train in the same methods
- They are the "boss-tier" enemy — encountering FJR 6 troops should feel distinctly
  different from fighting garrison troops
- Their poor transport means they arrive on foot, giving the player a time window
  before they concentrate
- FJR 6 took 75% casualties during the Normandy campaign — they fight hard but can
  be beaten

## 2. 91st Airlanding Division (91. Luftlande-Infanterie-Division)

**Commander:** Generalleutnant Wilhelm Falley (killed June 6, 1944)
**Strength:** ~7,500 men
**Quality:** MODERATE — newly formed but with a specific anti-airborne mission
**Key units:** Grenadier Regiments 1057 and 1058, plus supporting artillery and engineers

### Profile

The 91st was formed in January 1944 at Baumholder from replacement center personnel —
essentially a training division converted to combat status. It arrived in the Cotentin
in May 1944, giving it barely a month to familiarize with the terrain and prepare
defenses. Despite its "Airlanding" designation (suggesting training for mobile, light
operations against airborne threats), it functioned as conventional infantry.

Generalleutnant Falley was ambushed and killed by American paratroopers near Picauville
in the early hours of June 6 while returning from the war games at Rennes. His death
decapitated the division's command structure at the worst possible moment.

### Equipment

Light equipment typical of air-landing formations:
- Standard Kar 98k rifles
- MG 42 machine guns
- 8cm and 12cm mortars
- Limited artillery (Artillery Regiment 191)
- Minimal motorized transport — mostly horse-drawn
- No organic armor

### Morale & Readiness

Mixed. The division was new and untested. Its troops had not seen combat. Its officers
were competent but hadn't had time to build unit cohesion. The loss of Falley on D-Day
morning created confusion and delayed organized response.

However, the division had a specific anti-airborne mission and had been training for it.
Individual companies and platoons could fight effectively even without division-level
coordination — German doctrine emphasized subordinate initiative (Auftragstaktik).

### Gameplay Implications

- 91st Division troops are the "standard" enemy — competent but not elite
- Their response to the airborne drops is slower and more confused than FJR 6
- Loss of their commander creates a window of disorganization (reflected in readiness
  clock mechanics)
- Company-level and platoon-level units can still be dangerous — don't mistake
  division-level confusion for incompetence at the squad level
- They know the terrain — they've been patrolling it for a month
- Artillery Regiment 191 elements were stationed near Sainte-Marie-du-Mont (~60 troops)
  and represent the immediate local defense the player encounters first

## 3. 709th Static Infantry Division

**Commander:** Generalleutnant Karl-Wilhelm von Schlieben
**Strength:** ~12,000 men (spread across 250+ km of coastline)
**Quality:** LOW — garrison troops, static defense, limited mobility
**Sector:** Eastern and northern Cotentin coast including Utah Beach

### Profile

The 709th was a "bodenständig" (static) division — designed to hold fixed coastal
defenses, not maneuver. Its mission was to man bunkers, man beach obstacles, and wait.
Von Schlieben was an experienced Eastern Front commander given a garrison posting,
which tells you something about how the German Army viewed this division's role.

The critical weakness: the 709th's personnel were constantly being siphoned off to
the Eastern Front. Entire combat units (like the 1st Battalion, 739th Grenadier
Regiment) were transferred east and replaced with whoever was available.

### Composition Problems

- **Ost-Battalions**: Multiple battalions were composed of "Ostlegionen" — former Soviet
  POWs and Eastern European conscripts (Georgians, Poles, various nationalities) who
  chose fighting for Germany over starvation in POW camps. Led by German officers and
  NCOs, but motivation and reliability were deeply questionable.
- Two battalions of the 739th Grenadier Regiment were specifically Georgian battalions
- Three of eleven battalions were manned by former Soviet POWs
- Many remaining German personnel were older men, recovered wounded, or inexperienced
  conscripts with no combat experience
- The division the Germans themselves distrusted

### Equipment

- Basic infantry weapons (Kar 98k, MG 42) but older models common
- Fixed coastal defense guns and bunker-mounted weapons
- Horse-drawn transport exclusively — zero motorized capability
- Limited ammunition reserves for mobile operations
- Bunker/fortification weapons that cannot be moved

### Morale & Readiness

Low to very low. The Ost-battalion troops had minimal motivation to fight for Germany.
The German troops were garrison soldiers who had spent months or years in static
positions. High percentage had never heard a shot fired in anger. When the shooting
started, many either surrendered quickly or fled.

Von Schlieben was competent but his division was a shell. He later surrendered Cherbourg
after a brief siege — his troops simply couldn't hold against determined assault.

### Gameplay Implications

- 709th troops are the weakest enemy the player encounters
- They break more easily under fire, surrender more readily, and are slower to react
- Ost-battalion troops may surrender immediately when surprised, or fight
  unpredictably — they're desperate men in a bad situation
- Their fixed defenses (bunkers, beach obstacles) are dangerous but immobile —
  once bypassed, they're irrelevant
- They cannot pursue, flank, or maneuver — they hold ground or they break
- Encountering 709th troops early in the game should give the player false confidence
  before meeting the 91st or FJR 6

## 4. 352nd Infantry Division (Elements)

**Commander:** Generalleutnant Dietrich Kraiss
**Strength:** ~12,000 men total (only elements in the 101st's area)
**Quality:** MODERATE-HIGH — the best conventional infantry division in the sector
**Primary sector:** Omaha Beach (not the 101st's main area, but elements overlapped)

### Profile

The 352nd was Rommel's insurance policy. Formed November 1943, its cadre consisted of
hardened veterans from the 268th and 321st Infantry Divisions — survivors of the Eastern
Front. The division was positioned between the 709th and 716th Divisions, and its
presence at Omaha Beach was unknown to Allied planners, contributing to the catastrophe
there.

The 352nd's primary fight was at Omaha, but elements of its southern flank (particularly
the 914th and 915th Grenadier Regiments) extended toward the 101st Airborne's operational
area. These elements represented a potential threat from the south and east.

### Composition

- Junior and senior NCOs were Russian Front veterans — combat-experienced leaders
- Senior officers were experienced commanders
- Enlisted ranks were a mix: some experienced, many raw recruits, including very young
  soldiers and Volksdeutsche (ethnic Germans from Poland, Czechoslovakia, France)
- Soviet auxiliaries (Hilfswilligen) comprised over 25% of the division
- Anti-tank assets included 14 Marder II, 10 StuG III

### Equipment

- Full infantry division equipment — significantly better armed than the 709th
- StuG III assault guns — armored vehicles the 101st had limited means to counter
- Marder II tank destroyers
- Organic artillery
- Some motorized transport (but mostly foreign vehicles with unavailable spare parts)

### Morale & Readiness

Moderate to high. The veteran NCO cadre provided backbone. Training was intensive but
hampered by supply shortages — each grenadier had thrown only two hand grenades before
D-Day. Fuel shortages limited vehicle training. The division was prepared for defensive
warfare and trained specifically to repel waves of infantry while outnumbered.

### Gameplay Implications

- 352nd elements are a late-game threat — they don't appear until the Germans have
  organized and begun moving units laterally
- If encountered, they fight more effectively than 91st Division troops: better
  fire discipline, better use of terrain, more aggressive counterattacks
- StuG III or Marder presence would represent a serious escalation — the player has
  no armor and limited anti-tank capability
- The 352nd's arrival signals that the German defense is stiffening and the easy
  phase is over

## Force Quality Summary (Gameplay Reference)

| Unit | Quality | Aggression | Discipline | Reaction Speed | Surrender Threshold |
|------|---------|------------|------------|----------------|---------------------|
| FJR 6 | Elite | Very High | Very High | Fast | Very High (fights to near-destruction) |
| 91st Division | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate | Medium | Moderate |
| 709th (German) | Low | Low | Low-Moderate | Slow | Low |
| 709th (Ost-Bn) | Very Low | Unpredictable | Very Low | Very Slow | Very Low |
| 352nd Elements | Mod-High | High | High | Medium-Fast | High |

### Encounter Frequency by Game Phase

- **Act 1 (0100-0600)**: Mostly 709th garrison troops and scattered 91st Division patrols.
  Occasional FJR 6 patrol if near their sector. Artillery Rgt 191 at Sainte-Marie-du-Mont.
- **Act 2 (0600-0900)**: 91st Division units organizing. FJR 6 moving toward objectives
  on foot. 709th troops either dug in or broken.
- **Act 3 (0900-0100 next day)**: FJR 6 counterattacks. 91st Division coordinated defense.
  Possible 352nd elements arriving from the south. This is the hardest phase.
